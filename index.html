01

P&AI has to preserve business-unit speed while standardizing the areas where fragmentation creates cost, risk, poor data, or slow decisions.

“It probably feels like every standard comes with a tax on local speed.”
02

S/4HANA without operational paralysis

“Nobody wants an ERP program that is technically green and operationally red.”
03

M&A integration without disruption

The JD explicitly names IT due diligence, systems assessment, ERP and infrastructure integration, cyber risk evaluation, and operational continuity.

Four priorities, one operating cadence

What I would put on the table with the Division President and business leadership team.

Click a priority. Each one starts with a calibrated question, not a predetermined answer.

ERP value realization
“What would make an S/4 cutover feel like a business success—not merely an IT milestone?”

Build the program around process owners and measurable operating outcomes: order flow, schedule adherence, inventory accuracy, close cadence, data quality, adoption, and exception reduction.

Governance

Clear decision rights, design authority, risk cadence, stage gates, and escalation paths.

Business readiness

Process truth before configuration; super-users, scenario testing, cutover rehearsal, and floor-level adoption signals.

Value scorecard

Track realized operational and financial value after go-live, not just schedule and budget.

M&A integration factory
“What would have to be true for the next acquisition to integrate faster—with less disruption—than the last?”

Create a repeatable playbook with a light-touch Day 1 and disciplined migration path: protect revenue and continuity first, contain risk second, then sequence applications, data, infrastructure, and ERP based on value and dependency.

0–30 days

Cyber exposure, identity, asset inventory, critical systems, continuity risks, vendor and license obligations.

30–90 days

Data map, integration architecture, application disposition, KPI mapping, and ERP fit/gap.

90–365 days

Sequenced migration waves with synergy capture, business ownership, and continuity metrics.

Cyber + resilience
“How would we know—before an incident—that recovery plans and access controls work under pressure?”

Treat cyber as operational resilience: asset visibility, privileged-access discipline, vulnerability closure, tested recovery, incident roles, third-party risk, and clear business ownership of critical services.

See it

Know assets, identities, dependencies, data paths, and risk ownership across the portfolio.

Exercise it

Tabletops, restore tests, failover evidence, and executive decision rehearsals.

Measure it

Patch SLA, privileged access, recovery evidence, critical-service coverage, and closure aging.

Data + AI portfolio
“Which decisions become meaningfully better when the data is trusted—and which pilots should we stop funding?”

Move from idea volume to governed portfolio value. Start small, instrument the workflow, prove value, establish human accountability, and scale only where adoption and economics survive real operations.

Prove

Baseline the workflow, define the decision and metric, test with humans in the loop.

Govern

Data lineage, model boundaries, security, access, evaluation, escalation, and ownership.

Scale

Adoption, hours removed, cycle time, service speed, forecast quality, yield, and margin impact.

“You may be thinking: strong operator—but can he lead division IT in a federated model?”

That is a fair question.

I am an operator-builder who has spent a career where technology either lands—or stalls: engineering, factory, supply chain, quality, customer support, data, automation, and leadership cadence.

I will not pretend an S/4HANA transformation is a software install, or that the Division VP IT should out-SAP the architects. My job would be to make sure architecture, process ownership, data readiness, testing, cutover, adoption, and value capture all become one accountable operating system.

The question is not whether the ERP goes live. The question is whether the business gets better because it did.
